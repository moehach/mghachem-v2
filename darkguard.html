Darkguard

Preface

Full disclosure: If you're looking for a "serious" project, this is not the place for you. You'll find more "serious" work elsewhere in the portfolio. Now, if you're looking to have fun - This is the place to be.

UX doesn't have to be serious talk all the time. It can be used in completely made up scenarios that allow our imagination to run wild. I enjoy doing imaginary or unrealistic projects on the side because it allows me to experiment with different methodologies with little consequence. It also serves as a remind that underneath the serious face UX holds, there's a lot of fun going under the hood. So without further ado, I present to you: Darkguard. This is set of applications we're developing to help Bruce Wayne out in some of his more peculier night time activities. You might ask, Why are we helping Bruce?

We found out that Bruce Wayne is in fact Batman.

The billionaire playboy has been keeping this part of his identity hidden from plain sight. For years he has been trying to serve justice in the streets of Gotham with the help of Alfred and Robin. We want to lend Batman a helping hand. Bruce is an amazing technologist, but he's terrible at interfaces, creating user flows, and understanding his and his team's needs.

In this project our goal is to create an application that allows Batman to effectively fight crime by leveraging technology and police databases to make sure he shows up in the right place and the right time, while also effectively communicating with his team, ensuring their safety, and interfacing with other devices.

Due Diligence

Technical Requirements

Our goal is to design a mobile application that will make Batman's life easier and allow him to help more people.

Commissioner Gordon, from the Gotham Police Department, has given us access to GothamAPI. GothamAPI gives us real-time access to calls to the police station. By leveraging this technology we are able to analyze the source, location, and the identity of the person calling the station.

We also want to integrate GothamAPI with BatAI. BatAI is an artificial intelligence system. It is able to collect data ia GothamAPI to asses the severity, possible perpetrators, and deliver the latest updates of an event.

Solution #1: Call Superman

In a bid to create an effective solution, I've managed to reach a quick, (literally) bulletproof solution. All-Star Superman can fly at a speed of 186,000 miles per second. Gotham and Metropolis are only 180 miles apart and exist within the same universe.

Last I checked, Batman and Superman were also on good terms. If we connect Batman to Superman, we would get a response time of far less than a second. This would make this solution the fastest, low effort solution... Until Superman gets annoyed.

Now what happens if Superman gets fed up of Batman's constant calls? Or more realistically, what if Batman gets really stubborn and refuses to use our app to call Superman? I suppose that's where we come in and try find a more independent solution.

The User

You might think this section is completely unnecessary considering we already know our user is Batman, but to help Bruce, or Batman, we need to better understand who he (or they) is (are). This will allow us to gain a deeper insight as to where we fit in the equation, and where we´re needed the most.

If we dive deep into the psyche and persona(s) of Bruce Wayne, it becomes clear that we're not working with one persona, but rather three different personas, with each serving very different purposes.

Public Bruce

Bruce wayne puts up a facade when out in public where he wears the masks of an extrovert playboy with the ability to charm you, but is also a fair bit outlandish in his behaviour. This persona is the public face of Wayne Enterprises and has quite a bit of spotlight on him. Public Bruce is not purely an act, but it's definitely pushing the extremes of who Bruce is when he's on his own.

Batcave Bruce

Unlike Public Bruce, Private Bruce is a lot more reserved and does not want the spotlight on him. When public Bruce gives to charity, he wants to be the centre of attention, while private Bruce is far more altruistic and unconcerned for glory. If you watch his interactions with Alfred, you begin to see a much more intimate and approachable person with a lot of weight on his shoulders.

Batman

Batman encompasses both the worst and best aspects of Private and Public Bruce. Batman fo llows a stringent moral code when it comes to justice, and wants to instill fear in the heart of those that stand in his way. The difference between the Bruces and Batman however aren't as great as one might think since Batman is still altruistic, caring, and justl ike the other Bruces: Driven by vengeance.

Public facing Bruce might be where we are best able to help Batman.

Out of all three different personas, Public Bruce is the persona that might need to be kept in the loop. Public Bruce is busy keeping up an act, and a façade to hide Batman from the world. We need to be able to help Public Bruce stay in the loop with what is happening in the city, and help him figure out where he needs to focus his attention.

The Design Process

GothamAPI

We are designing a mobile app that helps Batman fight crime more efficiently. To get started, we ought to take a look at the available tools and how they behave on a higher level.

Commissioner Gordon has given us access to GothamAPI. GothamAPI gives us real-time access to calls to the police station. By leveraging this technology we are able to analyze the source, location, and the identity calling the station.

Crime Report Flow

Batman only ever needs to show up to crimes when their severity is ranked high up in the list. We need to be able to filter out petty theft, or crimes that have already been committed, and put in place a system that alerts Batman to situations where his intervention is needed.

After all, we can't always keep sending Batman to save a cat stuck on a tree.

Creating an Action Plan

When an incident has been identified, it is added to a list of incidents Bruce can access, and learn more about.

Not all incidents are equal, and as such incidents are ranked by their severity. This is where the BatAI kicks in where it streams information from GothamAPI and begins to parse and notifies Bruce of incidents that need Batman to intervene.

Prototype

Dashboard

In the dashboard screen, Batman can view the latest updates and incidents as they come in from GothamAPI. The information parsed by an algorithm incorporated within the BatAI that allows it to assign priority/severity levels to incidents.

Low priority incidents are not listed in the dashboard as they're typically cases that do not require Batman's attention.

Updates are provided on a case by case basis, with displayed updates prioritizing cases Batman is actively tracking. The registration process gives users two different flows. Users can either register through OTP or via social media.

Incident List

This section gives Batman a list of incidents sorted by priority, severity, and location. BatAI sorts the incidents in a manner that allows Batman to fight crime efficiently by factoring the aforementioned factors.

Batman can search the incident list either by incident number, suspects, or by severity.

While Batman is not notified of minor incidents, he's able to view them and change their priority levels when viewing the incident's situational report (SitRep).

Incident Map

Batman can choose to view incidents in map mode to visually understand where different incidents are located.

The incident location markers are also colour coded to match their severity levels.

Situation Report

The situation report (SitRep) gives Batman an overview of an incident.

Confirmed suspects are listed in the report, and Batman can receive live updates as they come.

At the bottom of the SitRep is a list of suggested actions. Batman can select the actions he wants to apply, and with a press of a button send notifications/instructions when beginning an investigation.

Batman is also able to change the status of a SitRep between Active/Closed/On-hold.

If Batman closes a case, he can choose to mark it closed manually through the status screen, or wait for GothamAPI/BatAI to automatically mark the case as closed.

If Batman taps on archive, he can choose to save the record for future reference.

Note: None of the changes or actions will affect the record as present within Gotham's police department.

Suspect Record

Batman can access the Suspect Record screen when a suspect's involvement with an incident is confirmed.

The record is pulled from the Gotham police department's archives to give Batman detail information about the suspect, their motives, and where they were last spotted.

This screen will allow Batman to understand who he's up against, and what precautions he might need to take.

Multiple Touch Points

Primary Function

The application will have two primary functions meant for different devices. The functions are complimentary to one another and together provide a tactical and operational aspect to the system.

There is the mobile application that will be use for Tactical Preparation. This is the application that we covered, which gives Bruce/Batman insight into the situation they're jumping into.

The second application plays a primary role in Operations through a tool such as a Smartwatch. The Smartwatch will be used when Batman is in action and and cannot afford to use a device that generates light, especially since most of Batman´s actions take place in the shadows.

Final Thoughts

Good experience design is not the same as designing a beautiful interface. We forget all too often that the experience is happening not on the phone but rather in our minds. Our mind is on a constant lookout for our well-being, even when all that we are doing is seemingly risk-free.

Making an interface easy to use, easy to understand, and easy to navigate will help set the user at ease. It tells them that you aren't trying to hide something from them.

Experience design is about setting the mind at ease and answering the questions a customer might have. You might answer these questions textually, visually, or psychologically, but what's most important is that you answer these questions honestly because a user will ultimately always know the truth sooner or later. Treating your users with respect and honesty will allow you to create a long-lasting relationship with them. Trying to cheat your users through dark UX patterns might work the first time, but they will quickly learn from that experience to never trust your platform again.

Good experience design, just like good design, is innovative, functional, clean, and most importantly: honest.
